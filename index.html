About Us

A library initiative started by people, for people in rural areas

A library initiative started by people, for people in rural areas

📖

Our Story

Deepstambh Gramin Granthalaya is a registered NGO based in Akola, Maharashtra. Founded with the vision of bringing the joy of reading to every village, we believe that knowledge is the true lighthouse that can guide communities out of darkness.

❤

Why We Exist

Rural communities in Maharashtra often lack access to quality reading material. Children grow up without libraries, limiting their imagination and learning. Deepstambh bridges this gap by establishing community libraries run by the people themselves.

🌟

Our Approach

We collect donated books, set up reading spaces in villages, and empower local volunteers to manage these libraries. Our model is sustainable, community-driven, and designed to foster a culture of reading from the grassroots level.

Our Mission

A Library in Every Village

A Library in Every Village

01

Collect Books

Donate readable books you no longer need. Every book finds a new home in a village library.

02

Establish Libraries

Set up community reading spaces in rural villages across Maharashtra, starting with the Akola region.

03

Empower Communities

Train local volunteers to manage libraries, organize reading sessions, and build a lasting culture of learning.

04

Transform Lives

Through reading, we aim to open new doors of knowledge, imagination, and opportunity for rural children and families.

Professional Background

Varsha Chopade holds many prestigious academic degrees and serves as an Assistant Professor at RCSS, focusing on Strategic Stakeholder Management. A multi-faceted professional, she is recognized as an author, writer, counsellor, and has undergone RJ training in Kerala. She conducted SWOT analysis of the Central Museum Library in Indore, MP, for which she received an excellent SWOT report certificate.

Author, Poet & Media

  • Authored six books and is a poet in both Hindi and Marathi
  • Special article writer for Saamana & Tarun Bharat (Maharashtra and Karnataka editions), focusing on social issues
  • Created a short documentary on the life of Dr. S.R. Ranganathan, screened at the Prime Minister's Office (PMO), Delhi on Librarian's Day
  • Part of the short film "Paperless Library"
  • Active YouTuber sharing informative content on social topics

Social Work & Environment

Media Coverage

Deepstambh's work recognized by leading Marathi newspapers

अजिंक्य भारत 30 November 2025 | Page 10

ग्रामपंचायत येळवणमध्ये दीपस्तंभ वाचनालय सुरू; गावासमोर नवा आदर्श

Deepstambh Library inaugurated at Gram Panchayat Yelvan under the Chief Minister's Samruddha Panchayatraj Abhiyan. The library was set up as a model initiative for the village, providing access to stories, novels, biographies, and educational books for children, youth, and adults alike. Prof. Varsha Chopade provided the essential books for the library.

लोकमत 03 December 2025 | Page 4

ग्रामपंचायत येळवणमध्ये नवे वाचनालय सुरू

Under the Chief Minister's Samruddha Panchayatraj initiative, a new library was started at Gram Panchayat Yelvan, Akola. Prof. Varsha Chopade provided the necessary books, while the Gram Panchayat supplemented with additional reading material. Sarpanch Kajal Dinesh Madavi and community members participated in the inauguration.
